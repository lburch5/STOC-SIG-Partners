Section 02

Firm Overview

SIG Partners is a Dallas-based, family-owned holding company that acquires and permanently holds great American businesses. Rather than run a fund with a defined exit, SIG places an experienced, personally invested CEO at the helm of each company on day one and holds for the long term.

Ownership Model

Permanent, family capital with no fund structure and no obligation to exit. SIG holds capital it can deploy without a mandate to sell or a deadline to return it, targeting an indefinite hold with a minimum horizon of roughly ten years. The firm describes itself as entrepreneurs rooted in American small-business ownership rather than private equity investors or bankers.

Target Profile

Founder-owned American businesses generating $4M or more in revenue and roughly $1M to $8M of earnings, led by owners who care about their teams, customers, and legacy after a sale. Recent acquisitions span home care, skilled trades, distribution, and light manufacturing.

Operating Model

A CEO-in-Residence program places vetted operators, who invest personal capital alongside the founding partners, into acquired companies. The CEO relocates and leads from day one while the outgoing owner transitions out over 60 to 90 days, supported by an Operating Partner and a three-person board.

Value Creation Approach

SIG preserves the brand, team, and operations, then builds: experienced leadership, professional management systems such as EOS, outside sales, and digital marketing. Growth comes from reinvestment and compounding under permanent ownership. The model traces to Pillar Health Group, which the founders grew from $6M to $130M in revenue.

Section 03

Portfolio Activity and Investment Trends

Observations drawn from the firm’s portfolio, acquisition cadence, and public company information.

01
Acquisition pace is accelerating into a maturing platform
Why this matters
SIG has acquired roughly 36 businesses in about five years, and the cadence is rising. The last two years include its most active stretch, with Kandel Brothers in April 2025, Heart of Humanity in July 2025, Gaddy Drug in December 2025, Snipps in January 2026, and Krista Care in May 2026.
02
Home care and healthcare services is the anchor vertical
Why this matters
Roughly a dozen platforms sit in home care and healthcare services, including Oxford HealthCare, Grace Unlimited, Heart of Humanity, CareMax, Serving Spirit, Pacific Opportunities, Odyssey, Access Care, Ultimate, and Krista Care. The thesis traces directly to the founders’ Pillar Health Group experience.
03
Skilled trades and home services form a second concentration
Why this matters
HVAC, roofing, plumbing, and refrigeration recur across the portfolio (Snipps, McCormick’s, Prospector, Advanced Control Solutions, Allen Roofing, McHale, East Bay), with RSP (Reliable Service Partners) emerging as a dedicated trades buy-and-build platform.
04
Distribution and light manufacturing recur by design
Why this matters
Kandel Brothers (electrical supply), Tracked Lifts, AH1 (food distribution), FlagCo, Neville Built, and Accutronics reflect the founders’ industrial-distribution background, including Sterling Group’s Construction Supply Group roll-up and Eaton.
05
Value creation is operator-led, not financial
Why this matters
Each company receives a resident CEO, an Operating Partner, and a three-person board. Tools such as EOS, outside sales build-out, and digital marketing (FlagCo) professionalize founder-run businesses rather than re-engineer their balance sheets.
06
A decentralized, permanent-hold structure
Why this matters
Businesses run independently from Alaska to Florida with brands and teams intact, and there is no exit clock. Value is intended to compound through reinvestment over a hold horizon of at least a decade rather than a hold-to-sell cycle.

Section 05

Portfolio Signals and Strategic Themes

Specific, evidence-based signals that connect portfolio observations to areas of potential relevance. Intended to inform conversation, not to sell.

01
A dedicated, multi-person business-development team drives an accelerating acquisition pace.
Why it matters
SIG’s sourcing bench spans a Senior Vice President and several associates and analysts running proprietary outreach at volume. As pace rises, throughput and target coverage become the practical constraint on how fast the holding company can grow, especially where proprietary deals compete with brokered flow.
Potential STOC Alignment  ·  CDS
Proprietary sourcing, market mapping, and targeted owner outreach embedded alongside the BD team and accountable to the pipeline.
02
Acquisitions are founder-owned businesses that often carry non-institutional financials.
Why it matters
Founder-built companies frequently arrive with cash-basis or unaudited financials that require normalization before a resident CEO underwrites the seat. With a large sourcing engine and a leaner diligence bench, analytical capacity on each file is where timelines tighten.
Potential STOC Alignment  ·  TAS
Quality of Earnings, EBITDA normalization, and net working capital analysis delivered on deal timelines and built for institutional scrutiny.
03
Thirty-six independently run businesses now sit under one permanent-capital holding company.
Why it matters
A decentralized base spanning home care, skilled trades, distribution, and manufacturing has no fund-level reporting cadence by default. Founders and Operating Partners benefit from durable, comparable KPIs to see performance consistently across very different industries.
Potential STOC Alignment  ·  FDI
Standardized KPI reporting, board pack design, and dashboards that give a consistent performance view across a multi-company portfolio.
04
A resident CEO lands in each business on day one and must establish a performance view quickly.
Why it matters
Newly placed operators standardize reporting and close processes in their first months, yet founder-built financial infrastructure is often inconsistent across the portfolio. A clean baseline early shortens the path to confident decision-making.
Potential STOC Alignment  ·  FDI · TAS
Post-close financial infrastructure, reporting cadence design, and a clean performance baseline for newly placed leaders.
05
Both the trades (via RSP) and home care scale through add-on acquisition.
Why it matters
Platform and vertical roll-ups depend on a steady flow of qualified, non-competitive targets within defined niches and geographies. Add-on sourcing is a different discipline from new-platform sourcing and rewards dedicated market mapping.
Potential STOC Alignment  ·  CDS
Add-on sourcing and market mapping within skilled trades and home care, complementing the platform pipeline.
06
Many portfolio companies are recruiter- or technician-driven local services businesses.
Why it matters
Organic growth in home care and home services hinges on demand generation, CRM discipline, and commercial reporting, the same marketing, sales, and AI systems SIG’s CMO is building across the portfolio. Consistent commercial infrastructure compounds across a decentralized base.
Potential STOC Alignment  ·  GES
CRM build-out, revenue operations, and commercial KPI reporting standardized across portfolio companies.
